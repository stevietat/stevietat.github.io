MEGA SHARK

a children’s story

Stephen Taubman

From an icy fortress,
a giant descends,
following the current,
he searches for friends.
Most call him Mega Shark,
which is deservedly grand,
for you won’t find much bigger
in air, sea or land.
But this makes it tricky,
you’ll soon start to see,
as most other sea-folk,
won’t come near easily.
The dolphins, the octopi,
the whales and the squid,
when Mega Shark comes,
make sure to be hid.
And try as he might
to extend a friendly fin,
I guess when you’re different,
it’s hard to fit in.
Thus we find Mega Shark,
our large lonely swimmer,
when SUDDENLY above,
he catches a glimmer.
Up past the ocean,
far in the sky,
‘swims’ a large creature,
to Mega Shark’s eye.
“He won’t be scared,”
Mega Shark thought.
“I’ll just jump out to meet him,”
so out the water he shot.

did he

MAKE IT?

No, he didn’t succeed.
What he then realized,
was he needed more speed.

And so,

HE DIVES

Down, down,
Mega Shark races,
past coral and starfish,
that shift in their places.
Deeper and deeper,
past large killer whales,
past schools of tuna,
and past fish with sails.
Rocketing downwards,
like a bullet he streaks,
scattering squid,
who click with their beaks.
Now deep in the darkness,
he halts his descent,
while small bubbles rise,
from volcanic floor vents.

And then,

HE RISES

Up, up,
Mega Shark goes,
past jellyfish that radiate
with magical glows.
Upward and onward,
faster he hurtles,
past hammerhead sharks,
and deep sea turtles.
Into lighter waters,
he turns on the throttle,
then blasts out the sea,
like a cork from a bottle.
He flies through the air,
leaving the ocean behind,
past flocking seagulls,
and their screeches unkind.
Up through the clouds,
to where the large creature soars,
he’s sure he can make it,
just a little bit more!
Though he starts to slow down,
his heart urges him on,
for the hope of new friendship,
is simply too strong.
Just a few inches more,
though gravity tries to forbid,
Do you think he made it?

Oh yes,

HE DID.

THE END
